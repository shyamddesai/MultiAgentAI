Analysis Report

Highlights

In the natural gas market, August Nymex natural gas futures have been declining, falling below $2.500/MMBtu, driven by high production levels, milder weather forecasts, and reduced demand outlooks. The EIA reported seasonally light storage builds, and legal decisions, such as the DOE lifting an LNG permit pause, have influenced market dynamics. Weather forecasts and regional heat waves have caused fluctuations in cash prices, particularly in the West and Texas. Freeport LNG's slow restart and operational issues, along with increased U.S. LNG feed gas demand, have further impacted the market. In the crude oil market, Asia's crude oil imports declined in the first half of the year, contradicting previous bullish forecasts and potentially impacting global demand. Strong market fundamentals, lower crude exports from OPEC and Russia, and increased refinery runs have driven oil prices higher, with Brent prices rising by over 6% in June. US oil production forecasts have been lowered from August onwards, supporting higher Brent prices. External factors, such as early hurricanes and the Federal Reserve's interest rate decisions, are also influencing market trends. Energy executives forecast a more bullish outlook for natural gas prices by early 2025, reflecting a longer-term positive sentiment.
